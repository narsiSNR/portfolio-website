Project 1

BD COR GX Shuttle fixture
01

What?

    Designed and fabricated a spring-based shuttle fixture for BD COR GX/MX systems, replacing tube-based testing (100–500 cycle limit) to enable 20,000+ cycle reliability testing without engineer intervention.

How?

  • Designed in SolidWorks with GD&T; fabricated via 3D printing and CNC.
  • Sourced components from Lee Spring and McMaster-Carr and built 10 fixtures for testing.
  • Evaluated CNC-machined contact plates in UHMWPE, ABS, SS, Al, and PEEK.

Result!

  • Characterized tube puncture force profile using custom test fixture and Shimadzu.
  • Validated UHMWPE plate to match tube force profile within ±10% with various springs.
  • Confirmed consistent performance of UHMWPE plate through 20,000+ cycles.

Project 2

Bilayer hydrogel microneedle drug delivery system for targeted therapy
02

What?

    Designed and fabricated a bilayer activated hydrogel microneedle device for targeted drug delivery in the GI tract, targeting chronic conditions like Crohn's disease. Optimized fabrication time and tuned unfolding behavior to control drug delivery timing.

How?

  • Fabricated PDMS microneedle stamps using metal syringe needles, Formlabs SLA, and BMF 3D printing across geometries (cylindrical, star-Shade, flower, helical spring).
  • Applied DOE and Minitab to identify key factors controlling hydrogel unfolding time and microneedle penetration depth in agarose intestinal surrogate.
  • Developed custom Python code (OpenCV, NumPy) to automate video analysis for radius of curvature and penetration depth.

Result!

  • Reduced fabrication time from 2 hours to 10 minutes consistently.
  • Pinpointed hydrogel concentration and device dimensions to achieve 3-minute activation.
  • Cut video processing time from ~1 week to a few hours, replacing manual ImageJ workflows.

Project 3

Material characterization using TEM (JEOL F200)
03

What?

    Academic project using TEM (JEOL F200) to identify and differentiate two visually similar materials deliberately ground and mixed into a single homogeneous sample.

How?

  • Prepared sample via drop casting onto a copper TEM grid.
  • Used low/medium magnification imaging to map particles and flag regions of interest.
  • Applied high magnification imaging to resolve distinct material regions.
  • Captured selected area diffraction patterns; calculated d-spacing and matched against ICSD database for structural identification.
  • Performed STEM bright-field/dark-field imaging for nanoscale structural details.

Result!

  • Successfully differentiated Lizardite and Albite within the mixed sample using EDX mapping.
  • Identified an unplanned contaminant, Goethite, during medium magnification imaging, confirmed via EDX mapping and diffraction patterns.

Project 4

Design and validation of E-Bus for BHEL
04

What?

    BHEL project to design and validate an electric bus for employee commuting and transport, compliant with automotive and road/transport standards including AIS-038 and AIS-156.

How?

  • Designed complete E-bus in NX CAD via reverse engineering from existing STEP files, covering chassis, outer/inner panels, insulation, battery pack, wheel and axle, steering, suspension, seating, and floor panels.
  • Generated mesh in HyperWorks on chassis with loading conditions from battery, vehicle, and maximum passenger weight.
  • Performed static structural analysis using Ansys, Abaqus, and NX Nastran.

Result!

  • Achieved 15% weight reduction through iterative design optimization.
  • Validated final chassis structural integrity via simplified crash test simulation in Ansys.
